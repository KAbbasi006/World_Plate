Thai Cuisine

Known for its harmonious blend of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors. Dishes like pad Thai, green curry, and tom yum soup showcase fresh ingredients like lemongrass, chilies, and coconut milk. Herbs like basil and cilantro add layers of complexity, making Thai food globally celebrated for its vibrant flavors.

Famous Thai Dishes

Thai cuisine is celebrated for its balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors. Pad Thai, a stir-fried noodle dish with shrimp or chicken, is a popular choice around the world. Tom Yum Goong, a spicy shrimp soup with lemongrass and lime leaves, is known for its aromatic, tangy broth. Green curry is a creamy, coconut-based curry that's often made with chicken and fresh herbs. Som Tum is a refreshing green papaya salad with chili, peanuts, and lime.

Pad Thai

Stir-fried rice noodles with tofu, eggs, bean sprouts, and a tangy tamarind sauce, often garnished with peanuts and lime.

Tom Yum Soup

A hot and sour soup with lemongrass, lime, chili, and vegetables, featuring bold, refreshing flavors.

Green Curry (Kaeng Khiao Wan)

A rich coconut milk-based curry with green chili paste, fresh herbs, and tender vegetables, offering a mild yet flavorful heat.

Mango Sticky Rice

Sweet, sticky rice served with fresh mango slices and drizzled with coconut milk.

Thai Fried Rice (Khao Pad)

Fragrant rice stir-fried with eggs, vegetables, and herbs, offering a simple yet satisfying flavor perfect as a side or main dish.
